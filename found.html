Falls vs Finds


An important distinction in meteorite classificaton is finds vs falls. "Finds", or a found meteorite, is a meteorite which was discovered on the ground and recognized as being nonterrestrial in origin. Conversely, "falls" are meteorite samples that are observed in the atmosphere and then successfully tracked to the impact site where a sample is encountered.

This dataset shows that a majority of meteorite samples are found without corresponding to an astronomical observation. This indicates that any conclusions based on the "year" data should be considered very preliminary in the absence of additional geologic data that would narrow down a more exact time of impact. In the absence of such data, the "year" data must be considered to be the year found rather than the year fallen.
