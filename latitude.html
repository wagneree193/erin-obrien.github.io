Relationship of Latitude to Impacts


This analysis looked for a correlation between latitude and meteor mass to determine if certain parts of the globe are at a higher risk of a large impact. There does appear to be two clusters of an increased number of obsersvations as well as increased observed mass between -15 degrees and -40 degrees, and 25 degrees and 50 degrees latitude.

The observed trends cannot be interpreted as conclusive evidence that these latitudes are at a higher risk of meteor strikes due to the fact that they also correspond with the most populous latitude ranges on the planet. A greater amount of potential observers in an area must be considered as a factor that could introduce bias into the dataset.
